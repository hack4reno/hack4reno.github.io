Hack4Reno is a weekend hackathon event for building apps that create a more informed and engaged community in the city of Reno.

FAQ

Internet? Power? Monitors?

The wifi is provided by the Collective. The SSID and password are provided in the venue. Please be gentle. Power is located on/under the tables. The monitors are the property of the Collective and are for the use of its paying members only.

Computers?

The event is strictly BYOD. A laptop computer is recommended. Wireless internet is provided.

Can I leave?

Yes. The Collective will be open during the hackathon hours, but feel free to leave and come back as needed.

Food?

Meals provided: Saturday lunch and dinner, Sunday light breakfast and lunch. Snacks/drinks/coffee will be provided throughout the event.

We make an effort to accommodate different dietary preferences, but please ask if you’re not sure. We apologize in advance if the provided food doesn’t fulfill your specific needs - there are some great places to eat nearby, if you prefer.

Bathrooms?

Out the front door of the Collective, up one floor in the elevator.

Do I have to write a new project from scratch?

No, though many teams do. If you are working on an existing project, please be up-front in your presentation as to what was done during the hackathon. Some successful projects can be done without coding at all - local wiki, for example.

Do I own my project? Could I keep developing it? Could I sell it?

Any code written during Hack4Reno belongs to its creator. We don’t have any say in what you do with it after the event, but we hope that you’ll keep developing your ideas and get others to use them.

Can I code all… night… long?

Sure. It’s also likely a group will retire to one of the close local establishments for some wind-down at 10. It’s like that Semisonic song - you don’t have to go home but you can’t… stay… here.

Do I have to be a developer/programmer to participate?

No! Successful teams usually have a variety of roles. Designers and artists are sought after. Non-technical participants can often help in novel ways on projects. The most important thing needed is a desire to work together and make something great.

Do I have to be from Reno to participate?

Of course not - Sparks is fine, too. Just kidding. We won’t be checking your home address. The event is primarily a civic hackathon for the benefit of the greater Reno/Sparks/Truckee Meadows area. If your participation will benefit this community, we’re glad to have you - thank you so much for joining us!

Can I just come watch? Can my friend/partner come?

Yes! The event is open to the public. A great time to join us would be for the final presentations at 4pm on Sunday. Dropping in any other time is fine, but you may just see a bunch of people hunched over their laptops. Please be courteous - they’re working on great stuff!

Presentations?

We’ll have a projector available with a variety of hookups. It will be set up early to give you a chance to try out your equipment to work out any issues. Presentations will be quite short, so try to showcase your work quickly without getting bogged down in details.

Social media?

Hack4Reno is on Facebook and Twitter - use hashtag #hack4reno

Hackathon advice?

If this is your first hackathon, try to team up with someone who has done one before. Experience helps a lot.

Try to get a minimum viable product running first, then add features to it. That way, if you run out of time, you still have something to present!

Use what you know. Trying to learn new things during the hackathon can really slow you down. If your team is using an unfamiliar technology, try to pair up with a teammate that has experience.

Make something and present it. Showing anything is better than showing nothing and your efforts will be appreciated. Even if you think it is terrible, there's always the raffle!

Most importantly - relax, meet some new people, and have fun.
